dangerous influence

The phrase "dangerous influence in space" refers to a potentially harmful or adverse impact or effect within the context of outer space. This could include factors such as space debris, radiation, celestial phenomena, or other elements that pose risks or threats to spacecraft, satellites, or astronauts.




Rogue planet

A rogue planet, also known as a free-floating planet, is an exoplanet that does not orbit any star and instead drifts through interstellar space. These planets can vary in size, from Earth-sized to massive gas giants. They are often challenging to detect because they do not emit their own light. Rogue planets may form either through ejection from their parent star systems or by independent formation in interstellar space, making them intriguing objects for scientific study. Despite their extreme cold and lack of a host star, they provide valuable insights into planetary formation and evolution.

Asteroids and comets

Asteroids are rocky objects found in the asteroid belt, while comets are icy bodies with tails, typically originating from the Kuiper Belt or Oort Cloud.

Spacecraft are vehicles designed for travel or operation in outer space, and they come in various types, including satellites, probes, and crewed spacecraft. They are used for exploration, communication, scientific research, and more. Spacecraft can be robotic or crewed, and they are equipped with instruments and systems tailored to their specific missions. Numerous spacecraft have been launched by various space agencies and organizations to study celestial bodies, collect data, and advance our understanding of the cosmos.
